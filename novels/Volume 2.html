I - Spaceslinger

“What is that?” Elias yelled, casting a worried glance at the strange stone statue. The helicopter hovered steadily above the Indonesian soil, allowing for Director Ozim to get a better view of the issue. There appeared to be another cosmic anomaly that Director Ozim wanted to inspect with Elias.

“That is what is known as the Indonesian Eyes,” Ozim explained. “Nobody knows where it came from and what its purpose is,”

Ozim ordered the pilot to descend, with the two men landing safely on the ground. As they reached closer to the statue that was placed in the center of public view, a strange, distant energy signal emitted from the eyes of the statue. It wasn’t normal. Although it depicted that of a monk of some sort with the bald head and rustic robes it wore, it felt eerily out of place, not belonging to the correct culture and history.

After all, Elias did graduate with a masters in Anthropology. He understood human nature and this was nothing like what he had experienced before. Suddenly, a ghostly presence summoned within the statue, emitting like a hologram as it spewed itself from the mouth of the statue.

“Greetings cosmic entity…I see you possess the power of the Jespans…Your presence has revived me, marking my mission to begin!” the hologram laughed.

Director Ozim jumped backwards, alerted by the sudden emergence of this entity. “Who are you? What is this mission that you require to begin?”

The hologram took the form of the exact monk the statue had depicted, its translucent nature becoming more opaque as it descended onto the ground. “My name is Magha, a servant of the great Jespan Lords. I will not disclose my mission, so I will be gone!”

Magha extended his ghostly arms into Elias’s body, ripping his powers away from him. Elias tried to pull away and attack Magha, but due to his ghostly form, all his attacks passed him and didn’t register. He was somehow undead, stripping his powers away.

“Fool!” Magha snickered. As Elias’s power drained, Magha violently tore his arms back out of Elias’s body, leaving him drowsy, with minimum power. Elias’s vision blurred, stumbling as he took a step back.

“Elias!” Director Ozim yelled, helping him walk. Quickly, the two rushed to the helicopter ready to escape.

“Leaving so soon?” Magha questioned, grinning mischievously. As the helicopter rose back into the air, the nearby huts collapsed instantly as a humongous skeleton arose from the ground, tearing the Earth.

“My lord! An undead necromancer!” Director Ozim exclaimed, terrified at the sight. Suddenly, the skeleton began to collapse in on itself, creating a large explosion that flung out his bones. Instead of flying randomly, the bones aimed towards Elias, knocking him out of Ozim’s grasp and sending him flying out into the sky, in a completely different direction. “Elias!”

Ozim’s words rang in Elias’s ear as he felt the wind rush against his body. He was weak, unable to do anything. He flew through the air, carried by the bones to a distant place. Where? He didn’t know. All he knew was that he felt the cool night breeze brush past him, hinting at the possibility of traveling through the time.

The morning sun cast ethereal glows among the small tribal village that resided within East Timor. It was warm, the sunlight had bathed the soft green grass of the rolling hills that carried a dense barrier of natural forest. It was far from where Elias had first come from, that being suburban areas of Indonesia. Elias rubbed his eyes, waking from his painful slumber.

He couldn’t feel the cosmic energy anymore. He couldn’t feel his power flowing through his body anymore. There was no feeling, there was no power. It felt different. No, it wasn’t anything supernatural, but he felt human again. Just numbing pain.

The soft glow around his vision subtly blurred, enlarging the dense view of the landscape. Slowly, light seeped in, revealing the enchanting environment. It was strange for Elias to have woken up in a strange place, and he had no clue on where he was and how long he was knocked out for. Wherever he was, he needed to return back to base. The International Supernatural Intelligence Division needed him.

He sat up weakly, pushing against the soft grass. He was alone but not afraid. A soft, small shape pressed against his body and Elias glanced around. There were four, radiant creatures that emitted a cosmic glow, casting their innocent expressions up towards Elias’s grim frown.

“What…what are you?” Elias muttered. “You feel like my power…”

The creatures nodded.

“You’re…my power? Fragments of it?”

The creatures nodded again, in sync. They were companions to say the least, perhaps even as unbreakable pieces of Elias’s own body. It felt surreal, but the existence of these creatures was enigmatic and relieving. Was there still hope for Elias to regain his powers?

The creatures began to ecstatically jump around, enthusiastic to perform. What was their true nature? Elias still couldn’t figure out what exactly they were. The uneasy tension was still unbroken between them, with Elias being somewhat skeptical to accept them for who they were.

Instead, the creatures jumped carelessly onto Elias’s body as if they were his pets, hopping up and down.

“What can you do if you’re my power?” Elias asked. The creatures stared at Elias for a moment, then motioned for him to throw them into the air. “Me? Throw you?”

Elias hesitated for a moment, then reluctantly threw the blue creature into the sky. As it sped through the air, the creature’s body transformed into a majestical shape, depicting that of a serpent or dragon. It sliced through the air, spewing out a gelatinous liquid. Its body was quite strange, for it seemed to be made of pure liquid, water perhaps. Elias was astonished, he couldn’t believe such strange creatures had possessed such unique powers. The ability to transform into magical beasts? Insane.

Moreover, it seemed that the four creatures had represented the four elements of water, fire, earth and air. If Elias knew anything, it was that these creatures were going to be having a crazy journey. He decided at that moment, something had happened. An urgent matter required his attention.

“I need to get home! Or, we need to take Magha down!” Elias explained, now gathering the creatures together. “While I still have you all, let’s get home safely and beat everything that tries to get in our way!”

Elias sprinted towards the village that was located near where he woke up. Instead of finding an intact, stable town, the village was engulfed in flames, destruction was everywhere. Locals were screaming, and something must have happened.

“Magha!” Elias muttered.

“Are you okay?” Elias asked the locals, aiding them by putting out the fires with his water creature.

“No! Some egghead destroyed our village!” locals complained, describing the appearance of the terroriser. Elias concluded from the depictions the locals had outlined to be Magha and no one else. Only he had the ability to summon the souls of skeletons as he recognised scrawny bones appearing from the ground.

“Of course…It's him…” Elias grumbled. It didn't surprise Elias, as Magha was the one who stripped him of his power. Angry, Elias began taking down the skeletons with his newly equipped creatures.

“Taste my creatures!” Elias yelled, slinging his arsenal of creatures towards the skeletons. Quickly, they broke apart and a pile of bones remained on the ground. With his fire creature, Elias burnt them all to ash.

“Is everyone alright?” Elias asked, checking up around the village. Using his creatures, he helped rescue any stuck residents, helping everyone safely.

“Thank you boy…those creatures of yours look rather similar to etchings from a nearby temple…” a local suggested.

“Really?”

“Yes, go through the forest and it should be on the other side,”

“Thank you so much!” Elias smiled, bolting off. Similar etchings in a temple? Was this another Jespan structure on Earth? He couldn't believe it.

As he darted through the trees like a maze, skeletons rose from the solid ground. Again. Each tree Elias weaved through he managed to throw a creature, directed towards each skeleton that rose to challenge him. It wasn't a challenge, but keeping his momentum throughout was difficult.

A clearing centered in his vision suddenly, for an open glad to have appeared in front of him was quite a miracle. Was this the temple? Suddenly, two skeletons grasped his shoulders, pulling him down. As a last ditch effort, Elias threw his air creature into the sky, but it didn't hit anything.

“Stupid skeletons, get off me!” Elias yelled.

As he waved his arms and hands around, he saw the air creature change directions in the air, realising he had full control over them all this time. Freely controlling the creatures changed his perspective as he raised his arms, allowing for the air creature to sweep the two skeletons in the face, knocking them out in one action.

“Nice!” Elias exclaimed, rushing forward. Indeed, there was a temple tucked away in the glade, a moss-ridden pyramid-shaped temple stood solitude in the woods. It was ominous to say the least, and Elias quietly snuck in. If anyone was to be here, it would be Magha. What was he after?

“Well, well, well. Look who's back for more!” Magha roared, jumping Elias as he stepped out from the shadows the entrance had captured.

Elias jumped back, trying to push Magha back. He forgot for a moment, he was a ghost and fell to the ground backwards instinctively, rolling away from his dangerous hologram.

“Die!” Elias yelled. He began slinging the cosmic creatures at Magha, weaving them through the skeletons he had started summoned. The skeletons were fragile now that Elias understood his power. The skeletons fell one by one, with multiple of the creatures landing a direct hit on Magha. Magha endured the hits, attempting to tank the damage. He didn't flinch nor panic.

“Is that all you can do? Magha mocked. He summoned a skeleton right before Elias, knocking him back. Elias was sent flying backwards violently into the wall, crashing with impact. “Hahaha!”

Magha sneered, laughing like a child. As he laughed, the final cosmic creature hit him. He flicked it off, thinking nothing of it. “The ritual is over! My mission is complete! Now to eliminate you…”  

“You had already lost the moment the cosmic creatures touched you…” Elias smirked through his gritted teeth.

“Eh?” Magha looked confused. Then, his stomach began to grow. His size slowly increased, his stomach bloated, followed by his body turning into something of a balloon. “What? Impossible!”

Magha's fingers had left the statues, interrupting the ritual. He rolled around the room like a ball, growing larger by the second.

“You are overflowing with cosmic energy…even if you are dead, spirits carry cosmic energy…But they have special properties…” Elias explained, wiping the blood off his face. His powers seeped back into his body, a nostalgic feeling entering his blood.

“This can't be…My energy levels…They're going beyond! Us spirits can only take so much! I'm gonna implode! I'm gonna–”

Magha was cut off by a blinding white light, a burst of cosmic energy engulfing the room. His spirit faded, his presence gone. Magha was dead, now officially redirected out of the world. No longer a ghost, no longer a spirit.

A surge of cosmic energy reignited within Elias's body, granting his powers back. His creatures? They had disappeared. They were indeed fragments of his power.

Suddenly, voices rang from the entrance, footsteps growing louder and louder.

“Fool! What took you so long?” a voice yelled.

“Director Ozim!” Elias exclaimed, happy to see him. He was hoping for a hug or congratulations, but he was met with a stern look.

“Magha has revived the Lords! You were too slow!” Ozim yelled, his voice laced with worry and panic.

“What?” Elias replied, eyes widening. He felt a sickening sensation within his body. There were more cosmic deities, stronger than Magha? It felt impossible.

“We don't know where they are. These Jespan Lords…” Ozim began. He was furious, but also relieved that Elias was alive.

“Director, I had my powers taken…I didn't know how much faster I could've beat him,” Elias apologised, his tone quietening down.

“It's alright,” Ozim explained. Whatever stood in front of them was a new challenge. At least Magha was resolved, for nobody had to worry about his necromantic presence haunting anyone ever again. Nobody would ever see the dead again, and nobody would be stripped of their powers.

Ozim quietly congratulated Elias, giving him a much needed hug and a formal pat on the back.

“It's alright kid, we'll get them next time.”
